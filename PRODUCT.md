# PRODUCT

Single-page personal site for **Othavio Quiliao** — fullstack engineer,
Curitiba, Brasil. Authors `agent-bar` and contributes to open-source
tooling for developers and the agents they work with: `herdr`,
`mission-control`, `rtk`, `svelte-animations`.

The site is the portfolio. There is no separate "work" or "showcase" page. Each
typographic decision is the demonstration.

## Register

**Brand.** Design IS the product. Marketing and identity-driven surface, not a
working tool. Apply the brand register reference from the `impeccable` skill.

## Users

The reader is a developer who arrived from a link in a thread, a tweet, a CV,
or a recruiter chasing context. They have ~30 seconds. They are not browsing,
they are scanning.

Two sub-audiences:

- **Peer developers** (primary). They came from GitHub, a Discord, an omarchy
  channel. They want to see if this person is worth following or working with.
  Decision in 10 seconds, exit fast if it smells like a template.
- **Recruiters / clients** (secondary). They came from LinkedIn or a referral.
  They want stack confirmation and a way to reach out. Decision in 30 seconds.

The site optimises for the first group. The second group still gets what they
need (work links, contact, projects) without the site bending toward them.

## Product purpose

Prove craft through restraint. Show, do not list. The visitor leaves with one
clear memory: a strong typographic statement, five concrete projects, three
ways to reach the person. Not a wall of skills, not a hero with a gradient.

The site has no commercial conversion goal. Success = the reader bookmarks,
clicks GitHub, or shares the link.

## Strategic principles

1. **Confidence through scarcity.** No section exists "in case." If it adds
   nothing, it is cut. The page is one screen of content plus a quiet tail of
   meta-information.
2. **Typography as the demo.** The visitor experiences craft before reading any
   word. Scale, weight, tracking, and rhythm carry the entire expression.
3. **No card chrome.** Cards, shadows, blurs, backdrop filters, and decorative
   illustrations are banned. Borders are hairlines. The page reads like a
   printed editorial spread, not a SaaS dashboard.
4. **Honest terminal lineage.** The aesthetic borrows from terminals and print
   posters because the author lives in those mediums (omarchy, neovim,
   Noctua Core). It is not cosplay. Fake terminal animations, ASCII art
   decoration, and "typing prompts" are banned.
5. **Bilingual as content, not feature.** PT / EN switches the identity
   sentence, project descriptions, and section labels. Names, URLs, and dates
   remain in their canonical form. The toggle is a single inline control, not
   a settings panel.
6. **Dark first, light intentional.** Dark is the primary lens (late-night dev,
   omarchy theme aesthetic). Light is paper / ink, designed with equal care.
   Both are valid, neither is the default fallback.

## Voice and tone

- First-person rare. The page speaks ABOUT the work, not about the feelings.
- Declarative, present-tense sentences. No marketing verbs ("crafting
  delightful experiences"). No résumé verbs ("spearheaded", "leveraged").
- One sentence carries the entire identity. It must be specific enough to
  exclude 90% of fullstack engineers.
- Sectional labels read as code comments: `/* OPEN SOURCE */`,
  `/* ELSEWHERE */`. They are signals, not headers.

## Anti-references — REFUSE on sight

- **The current page in this repo.** Card with `border-border/70 bg-card/80
  backdrop-blur-sm`, ASCII field overlay, rotated grid background, vignette,
  placeholder copy ("[your name]", "Quiet interfaces. Durable systems."), and
  amber glow. This is the exact "AI slop" pattern to leave behind.
- **The Vercel-style developer template.** Centered hero, three feature cards
  with lucide icons, gradient text, "build, ship, learn" verbs.
- **Generic monospace dev portfolio.** Big ASCII banner, fake terminal typing,
  marquee of tech logos, `> npm install me` joke.
- **Notion-page aesthetic.** Blocky sections, emoji headers, default fonts.
- **Brutalism cosplay.** Chunky borders everywhere, safety-orange labels,
  rotated text for the sake of it. Brutalism is a discipline, not a sticker
  pack.

## Aesthetic references — STUDIED, not copied

- **dhh.dk** — absolute confidence in plain prose, no chrome at all.
- **oddbit.ai** — one screen, terminal prompt, three text links. Anti-portfolio.
- **ampcode.com/chronicle** — editorial discipline applied to a changelog.

The new site shares their refusal of decoration. It does NOT copy their
content shape, because the author does not (yet) publish writing.

## Scope

- Single route: `/`.
- A `/not-found` that obeys the same system in a single line.
- A static OG image generated from the same typography.
- No blog, no `/about`, no `/projects` index, no contact form.
- Future additions (writing, talks, longer project pages) are deferred until
  the author has matter to publish. The current site does not pretend to
  have what it lacks.
